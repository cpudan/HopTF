Attached is the HopTF 48-variant validation PDF.

Short version: the clean ClinVar panel has 24 benign and 24 deleterious missense variants, all coordinate-checked against local responder isoforms. The strict evaluator predicted 48 / 48 variants.

Benign variants are mostly stable: 23 / 24 are within +/-5% response change. Deleterious variants shift more negative on average, but the class separation is modest. AUROC using -percent_delta is 0.578.

I would treat this as exploratory evidence for weak directional sequence sensitivity, not decisive validation of the current PCA-centroid endpoint model.
